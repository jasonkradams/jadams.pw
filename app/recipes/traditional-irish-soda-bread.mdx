This method utilizes a preheated cast iron Dutch oven to trap steam, creating an artisan-style, crackling crust while preserving a beautifully moist and tender crumb inside. The addition of a small amount of butter coats the flour proteins, preventing toughness.

---

## Equipment Needed

- **Large Mixing Bowl**
- **Whisk**
- **Wooden Spoon** (or your hands)
- **Cast Iron Dutch Oven** (with a tight-fitting lid)
- **Parchment Paper**
- **Sharp Knife** (for scoring)
- **Wire Cooling Rack**

---

## Ingredients

| Quantity | Ingredient | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **4 cups** | All-purpose flour | Plus extra for dusting your work surface. |
| **1 ½ tsp** | Baking soda | Make sure it is fresh for the best rise. |
| **1 tsp** | Kosher salt | Enhances the flavor of the bread. |
| **2 tbsp** | Unsalted butter | Must be **cold** and cut into small cubes. |
| **1 ¾ cups** | Buttermilk | Must be **cold** and well-shaken. Full-fat preferred. |

---

## Instructions

**1. Preheat the Oven and Pot**

Place your empty cast iron Dutch oven (with the lid on) into your cold oven. Preheat the oven to **400°F (200°C)**. Allow the pot to heat up for at least 30 minutes so it gets screaming hot.

**2. Whisk the Dry Ingredients**

In a large mixing bowl, thoroughly whisk together the flour, baking soda, and kosher salt.

**3. Cut in the Butter**

Add the cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture. Using your fingers, quickly rub the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

**4. Add the Buttermilk**

Make a well in the center of your flour mixture and pour in the cold buttermilk. Use a wooden spoon or your hands to gently and quickly mix the dough until it *just* comes together. **Crucial step:** Do not over-mix or knead the dough like yeast bread. Overworking it will make the bread tough. It should look a bit shaggy and rough.

**5. Shape and Score**

Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured piece of parchment paper. Gently pat it into a round loaf about 1.5 to 2 inches thick. Using a sharp knife, score a deep "X" across the top of the loaf (tradition says this "lets the fairies out," but practically, it helps the heat penetrate the thickest part of the dough).

**6. Bake Covered**

Carefully remove the hot Dutch oven from the oven. Using the edges of the parchment paper as a sling, lower the dough directly into the pot. Put the lid back on and return it to the oven. Bake for **30 minutes**.

**7. Bake Uncovered**

Remove the lid and bake for another **10 to 15 minutes**. The bread is done when the crust is a deep golden brown and tapping the bottom of the loaf sounds hollow.

**8. Cool**

Transfer the bread to a wire rack. Let it cool for at least **30 minutes** before slicing, as it continues to cook on the inside while resting.
