---
title: "Traditional Irish Soda Bread (Dutch Oven Method)"
date: "2026-03-06"
tags:
  - Irish
  - Bread
  - Baking
excerpt: "Baked in a preheated cast iron Dutch oven to trap steam, creating a crackling crust while preserving a moist and tender crumb inside."
description: "This method utilizes a preheated cast iron Dutch oven to trap steam, creating an artisan-style, crackling crust while preserving a beautifully moist and tender crumb inside. The addition of a small amount of butter coats the flour proteins, preventing toughness."
difficulty: "Easy"
servings: 8
prepTime: "PT15M"
cookTime: "PT45M"
equipment:
  - Large Mixing Bowl
  - Whisk
  - Wooden Spoon (or your hands)
  - Cast Iron Dutch Oven with a tight-fitting lid
  - Parchment Paper
  - Sharp Knife (for scoring)
  - Wire Cooling Rack
ingredients:
  - quantity: 4
    measurement: cups
    product: all-purpose flour
    note: Plus extra for dusting your work surface.
  - quantity: 1.5
    measurement: tsp
    product: baking soda
    note: Make sure it is fresh for the best rise.
  - quantity: 1
    measurement: tsp
    product: kosher salt
    note: Enhances the flavor of the bread.
  - quantity: 2
    measurement: tbsp
    product: unsalted butter
    note: Must be cold and cut into small cubes.
  - quantity: 1.75
    measurement: cups
    product: buttermilk
    note: Must be cold and well-shaken. Full-fat preferred.
instructions:
  - title: "Preheat the Oven and Pot"
    instruction: "Place your empty cast iron Dutch oven (with the lid on) into your cold oven. Preheat to 400°F (200°C) and allow to heat for at least 30 minutes until screaming hot."
  - title: "Whisk the Dry Ingredients"
    instruction: "In a large mixing bowl, thoroughly whisk together the flour, baking soda, and kosher salt."
  - title: "Cut in the Butter"
    instruction: "Add the cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture. Using your fingers, quickly rub the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs."
  - title: "Add the Buttermilk"
    instruction: "Make a well in the center and pour in the cold buttermilk. Mix gently until the dough just comes together. Do not over-mix or knead — overworking will make the bread tough. It should look shaggy and rough."
  - title: "Shape and Score"
    instruction: "Turn the dough onto lightly floured parchment paper. Pat into a round loaf about 1.5 to 2 inches thick. Score a deep X across the top to help heat penetrate the thickest part."
  - title: "Bake Covered"
    instruction: "Carefully remove the hot Dutch oven. Lower the dough in using the parchment as a sling. Cover and bake for 30 minutes."
  - title: "Bake Uncovered"
    instruction: "Remove the lid and bake for another 10 to 15 minutes until deep golden brown and hollow-sounding when tapped on the bottom."
  - title: "Cool"
    instruction: "Transfer to a wire rack and let it rest for at least 30 minutes before slicing, as it continues to cook inside while resting."
---
